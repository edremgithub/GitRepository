Exploring The Unknown

The full extent of the world can either lie in what is known, or what is not. Since the beginning of known history, desire and temptation to uncover such mysteries have driven humans to outdo their current standards and challenge the limitations of security. With this natural fascination came limits such as a common fear of the unknown, and oftentimes a self-doubting that only leads to further questioning.

The intrigue of mystery and desire to expand one’s knowledge tempts the human mind more than the conformity of what is already known, leaving the question of “What if?” to covet curiosity rather than implore disquietude, while pursuing socio-economic and scientific desires to better understand the relationship of human nature with modern dilemma.

Mystery is a conundrum; one that will only inspire discomfort, chaos and intrusion of safety. A common opposition to intrigue is the idea that man fears what he does not know, therefore he must return to his sanctuary of tradition. Yet, as humans are faced with recurring issues and dilemmas, it is those who dare to question such restrictions that are able to present solutions. Due to religious, political or paranoiac reasons, renaissance society crafted the idea that in order to live comfortably, one must never question what is recondite.
